About

    What is Standard of Living?

    Standard of living refers to the quantity and quality of material goods and services available to a given population. It represents the material abundance of people in the area in which they live and is also one of the most important determinants of happiness or well-being (Boyle, 2021; Grossnationalhappiness.com, 2015). Improving living standards has long been an important goal in national plans in both developed and developing countries (RRHBA, 2021).

    What is the research gap on current Standard of Living visualisation projects?

    Research on Standard of Living is mostly from government reports and academic reviews. Lack of dynamic visualisation of the changes over space and time. The existing standard of living dynamic visualisations did not obtain findings and conclusions from viewers.

    What are we aiming to achieve?

    Dynamic and interactive well-constructed webpage on different aspects of United Nations SSDS indicator system to measure the change in living standards over space and time for London, enabling users to explore in detail. Conclusion on key findings at the end of the page to give users a space to compare and reflect.


Research Questions

  • How have the Living standards in London changed over space and time?


    The visualisation objectives in the project focus on three key areas. To provide a platform for demonstrating changes in living standards. Observation of more minor levels (urban level and regional level) Viewing living standards through different facets (4 indicator categories).

Key Findings

Employment

    The unemployment rate in London rose rapidly from 2008 and remained high for several years, recovering to the same level as before the subprime crisis in 2015 but resuming its upward trend due to the global epidemic in 2020.

    The number of people employed in the three main sectors of technology, transport and storage, and education has been increasing since 2000, showing a gradual expansion from the financial sector to the technology sector in London.

Homeless

Over time, the distribution of Homeless People is not concentrated in a few Boroughs and that the higher boroughs vary from year to year.

The HPI data is close to the same, meaning that there is a slight variation between boroughs and that it rises higher and higher at the same time over time. Homeless people dropped to a minimum in 2010 but then started to rise, reaching a peak in 2018 and declining.

Recycle and Emission

The waste recycle rate showed a noticeable overall increasing trend. The recycling rate varies between boroughs essentially. Generally, outer London has a higher recycling rate than inner London.

The overall emissions have been lowered with a rise in the waste recycling rate. A significant bounce back appeared in 2012. After that, the total CO2e emissions dropped at a higher rate than in previous years.

Health

During 2011-2018, the personal well-being score has been steadily increasing. Women’s healthy life expectancy per capita has become longer and longer as time changes, and men are the opposite.

Although the number of breast cancer patients is still rising in 2019, the proportion of women with breast cancer has declined. The number of road casualted people is declining every year, and people in the 20-39 age group need to pay more attention to road casualties.

Conclusion

  • The choice of living standards as the theme for 'City Transformation' offers a fresh perspective on the city. Cities are complex systems, and it is not easy to comprehensively picture them (Batty, 2017). As a whole, the lives of people in a city may be getting better over time. However, on a more homogeneous level, housing prices may be getting higher, people's health problems may be increasing. On a more detailed level, some places may be getting better, others not so much. A comprehensive and high-level assessment of change is not representative of how everyone in a city is doing, and we hope that those watching this project will also feel what we felt when we made it.
